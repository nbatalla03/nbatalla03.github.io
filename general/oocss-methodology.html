OOCSS Methodology

I recently attended a presentation that covered reusable HTML, CSS, and JS. During the presentation I was re-introduced to OOCSS, a CSS methodology that I have heard about in the past, but never really dug into.

I've been looking to challenge how I currently approach CSS, and OOCSS seemed like a good place to start.

What is OOCSS?

OOCSS, stands for Object Oriented CSS. It was a methodology pioneered by Nicole Sullivan to encourage code reuse, efficiency, and maintainability in CSS.

Final Thoughts

I believe OOCSS is a step in the right direction. However I suggest you take this new methodology and slowly apply it to your current way of writing CSS.

I certainly had my gripes with OOCSS, in particular, the lack of using location-dependent styles made my HTML more bloated. For now, I'll continue to use OOCSS and tweak it for my needs. I'll go over in more detail my personal gripes when I have a few more projects under my belt using this methodology.
